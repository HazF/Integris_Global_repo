Connecting to AC Power

Connecting to AC Power

We strongly suggest that you fully charge the Integris system's battery before first use.

1. Plug the DC cord of the AC adapter to the power connector at the back of the chassis as shown below.

2. Plug the female end of the AC power cord into the AC adapter and the male end to an electrical outlet. (See Fig 1.1 ) (See Integris system power connections)

Caution Before plugging the power supply into a power source ensure your power source is compliant with the power supply requirements detailed on the power supply. The unit requires power input voltages between 90 and 240V AC with a frequency of 50Hz or 60Hz. The power source should be capable of supplying a current of at least 5A.

Integris system power connections

Power is now being supplied from the electrical outlet to the AC adapter and onto your Integris system. You are ready to turn on the computer.

CAUTION: Use only the AC adapter included with your Integris system. Using other AC adapters may damage the computer.

Turning the Computer On and Off

1. Open the top cover by pulling the cover latch and lifting up the cover. You can tilt the cover forward or backward for optimal viewing clarity.

Opening the Integris system

Press the Power button ( ) at the rear of the keyboard. The Windows operating system should now start.

Integris power button

When you finish a working session, you can stop the system by powering off the Integris system or leaving it in Sleep or in Hibernation mode:

Power off: Use the Windows Start menu in the lower left and follow the shutdown procedure.

Sleep: Press the power button or use the Windows Start menu to put the computer in Sleep mode.

Hibernate: Use the Windows Start menu to put the computer into Hibernation mode.

Note: “Sleep” is the default setting of the power button. You may change what the power button does in the Windows Control Panel.
